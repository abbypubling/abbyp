7/23/2004

Okay, getting started on this thing. No overt details just yet ^^
A great deal of work on this is being done by many volunteers. I'll be sharing more about that as the days go on.
I want to thank them all individually when this is, err, over... I say this, but it's not something designed to "end."

Some of the team isn't sure all of this will - as they so crudely put it - "totally pan out." Respectfully, I disagree.
If their doubts were truly reasonable, they would've expressed those a long time ago. They are aware of this.
They are also aware that we're on the verge of something big. J is in full support, at least. For now.

In other news, one of the lab boys - let's call him T - is telling the others to be on the lookout for what he only defines as a "phallic error."
Interesting phrasing! Total oddball, that T, but when he's spouting off, it's usually a sign that something's up. He's a dreamer, not a liar.
I feel like whatever weird thing he says next does end up materializing in some form. It's like a riddle. Will look into the error with J later.

In the meantime, I do want to emphasize that this is not a weapon.
There's a small division of collaborators who have convinced themselves that this thing is, or even should be, a psychological tool.
I can't help but wonder what their vision is. Though it seems like their work isn't particularly different from the rest of the team, so for now, it's small potatoes.


7/26/2004

J has informed me of some testing she did, and it seems like there's an outside influence that could cause some trouble later on.
I was a little skeptical. We're super uptight about anti-contamination measures. Then I looked at the data.
I couldn't believe it.

What J had caught were traces of a parallel network of procedural ideas. What we examined was mostly different from our work, but exactly as fascinatingly outlandish.
At first I wondered if another team had either came to the same conclusions as ours, or if they were somehow inspired by us (mole?).
Unfortunately, there isn't enough personalized information in the current data to be sure. J's going to run a few more passes on the set, just to see what we might be missing.

T and B are working hard on figuring out that error T was on about. I expect the source of the error is located close to that parallel network.
That's mostly my little spiteful side talking, but the timing would line up. Our initial investigations didn't connect the two in any meaningful way.
I'm trying not to dwell on it. It's a poisonous idea.

In the meantime, you should expect a lot from me in the following weeks.
The production division has been working overtime - on their own volition - so lots of progress, but also lots of tricky things for me to work out.
J is planning to build some properly fun stuff for the project with me. I'll drip-feed some of this when I can.


8/19/2004

As much as I wanted to keep this diary more "persistent," many timesinks have hurdled me, as well as computer-related issues.
I don't feel it's necessary to go into details, but many of my programs were restricted due to what T called a "permissions mix-up."
I brought it up with him during supper yesterday, and he fixed it pretty quickly. Do not blame this mishap on him - the delay was only because of my own (failed) attempts to fix it.

In regards to my own equipment, I prefer to do things my own way whenever possible.
That said, T is genuinely the most robust person I've ever met when it comes to computer science. I should've asked him earlier, but I'm not perfect.
He also pointed out that N - who has been awfully silent since July - was the only one who had her permissions elevated during the mix-up.

I'll try not to delve into the drama of the project too much, but I will say that if she wanted to be covert about something she did, she should try harder.
I've been a little antsy ever since the parallel network J and I discovered the other week. Sabotage is not something either of us take lightly.
Right now, we're relying on T more than ever to catch these problems in the future. Keep in mind the mix-up was smaller scale than I'm letting on. He would've caught it sooner if it weren't.

In the meantime, paranoia. That's all I can say regarding this whole discovery. Paranoia, paranoia, paranoia.
Things are happening a little too quickly for me to process it all, so I would especially like to avoid having a sitdown with a rogue someone.
I'm not gonna let this get too deep into my head. We're going to keep moving forward as usual until that "error" gets figured out. That's when we shift course.


8/21/2004

After careful consideration, I had a talk with N. It was a quick exchange during lunch, but I was able to get the gist.
As it turns out, N has not logged into the main system since day one. All of her work has been performed within the physical world.
We went to check her computer after the fact. It adds up. Her install is essentially factory-new.

Normally, that would still annoy me. However, it lines up with N's claim that she isn't very tech-savvy. I understand completely.
T also speculated that her digital absence may have contributed to the outcome of the permissions glitch. I'm not totally convinced, but it makes sense.
For now, I've told N to continue to avoid using computers, just as a safety measure. And also as a means to see what else the absence could affect.

What I think is important, as far as increasing morale, is that all of this is about the journey - not the destination.
And while that's partially because this project is not designed to end...
It's also important because I'm trying to have as much fun on this project as possible. Keeping the team motivated is also part of that fun.

In the meantime, B's finally made the first steps towards getting a clearer understanding of the "phallic error."
I didn't initially grasp T's phrasing. After B's progress, now I do.
The idea signatures, quite literally, form the crude shape of a human penis.
